Effectiveness of Physiotherapy

Physical therapy has been found to be effective for improving outcomes, both in terms of pain and function, in multiple musculoskeletal conditions. A 2012 review found that the use of spinal manipulation by physical therapists is a safe option to improve outcomes for lower back pain. According to randomized control trials, a combination of manual therapy and supervised exercise therapy by physiotherapists give functional benefits for patients with osteoarthritis of the knee, and may delay or even prevent the need for surgery. Studies have also shown that surgical decompression treatment and physiotherapy are on par for lumbar spinal stenosis in improving symptoms and function, and that physical therapy, particularly manual therapy techniques focused on the neck and the median nerve, combined with stretching exercises, may be equivalent or even preferable to surgery for Carpal Tunnel Syndrome.
A 2015 review suggested that while spine manipulation and therapeutic massage are effective interventions for neck pain, electroacupuncture, strain-counterstrain, relaxation massage, heat therapy, and ultrasound therapy are not as effective, and thus not recommended.

About the author

Dr. Nischay Paul Thakur, after graduating from Lovely Professional University, Jalandhar, worked with the Honorable Governor of H.P, Bandaru Dattatreya, and Chief Minister Jai Ram Thakur in Shimla, H.P.

Thereafter Dr. Nischay worked with different Advance Physiotherapy Rehabilitation Clinics like Mission Walk in Hyderabad, Spinofit plus in Pune, and Aadhya healthcare in Delhi.

After learning the core of rehabilitation he planned on starting his own venture in his home state Himachal as there was a lack of Physiotherapy Rehabilitation Clinics in a state where it was really needed, as statistics clearly indicated that people in Himachal suffer a lot from Orthopaedic issues, due to the rough terrain of the province.

He's full of enthusiasm and extremely thankful to be able to provide his advanced approach to his patients, and bring about a change in people's perception of physiotherapy.
